## Summary

Strategy 3 prioritizes small, high-density transactions. Among items with equal fee-per-cost, it prefers the smallest (lowest virtual cost) items first. This maximizes the number of transactions that fit within the block cost budget, which can yield higher total fees when many small high-FPC transactions are available.

This strategy complements the density sort ([SEL-003](SEL-003.md)) by breaking ties in favor of compactness rather than absolute fee. In scenarios where the mempool contains many similarly-priced transactions, compact sort can pack more items and extract more total fees.

---

## Specification

### Sort Order

Candidates are sorted by the following keys, in order:

| Priority | Field | Direction | Rationale |
|----------|-------|-----------|-----------|
| 1 | `package_fee_per_virtual_cost_scaled` | Descending | Highest density first |
| 2 | `virtual_cost` | Ascending | Smallest items first (pack more) |
| 3 | `fee` | Descending | Higher fee wins ties |
| 4 | `height_added` | Ascending | Older items win ties (fairness) |
| 5 | `spend_bundle_id` | Ascending | Deterministic tiebreaker |

### CPFP-Aware Greedy Accumulation

Identical to [SEL-003](SEL-003.md) greedy accumulation:

1. Compute unselected ancestors.
2. Compute total cost = item + unselected ancestors.
3. Compute total spends = item + unselected ancestors.
4. Skip if total cost > remaining block cost.
5. Skip if total spends would exceed `config.max_spends_per_block`.
6. Skip if any conflict with already-selected items.
7. Otherwise include item + all unselected ancestors.

### Difference from Strategy 1

Strategy 1 ([SEL-003](SEL-003.md)) sorts by `FPC desc, fee desc, virtual_cost asc`. Strategy 3 swaps the fee and virtual_cost tiebreakers: `FPC desc, virtual_cost asc, fee desc`. The primary key (FPC) is the same, but among equal-FPC items, Strategy 3 prefers the smallest item while Strategy 1 prefers the highest-fee item.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Candidates are sorted by package FPC descending as the primary key.
- [ ] The secondary key is virtual_cost ascending (smallest first).
- [ ] Remaining keys are fee desc, height_added asc, bundle_id asc.
- [ ] Greedy accumulation automatically includes unselected ancestors with child.
- [ ] Items exceeding remaining cost budget are skipped.
- [ ] Items exceeding spend count limit are skipped.
- [ ] Conflicting items are skipped.
- [ ] The sort is deterministic.

## Implementation Notes

- **Primary file:** `src/selection/strategies.rs`
- **When compact wins:** This strategy outperforms density and whale sorts when the mempool has many small transactions at similar fee rates. Packing more small items can yield more total fees than including a few large items.
- **Example:** Budget = 100M. Strategy 1 might pick: 1 item at cost=60M, fee=6M, FPC=100, then 1 item at cost=40M, fee=3.5M, FPC=87.5, total = 9.5M. Strategy 3 might pick: 5 items at cost=20M each, fee=2M each, FPC=100, total = 10M.
